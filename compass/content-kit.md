## English drafts

### 🌍 Extreme Poverty

```
🌍 Extreme Poverty: Roughly 800 million people live on less than $3.00 a day

A common misconception: "Global poverty keeps getting worse." The opposite: extreme poverty has seen the steepest sustained decline in human history over the last generation. The work is unfinished, not failing.

What the evidence actually points to: Direct cash transfers — Giving money directly to the poorest households, unconditionally. Roughly $1 delivers ~$0.85–0.90 into a family's hands.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/extreme-poverty.html
```

### 🦟 Malaria

```
🦟 Malaria: Roughly 600,000 deaths a year — most of them children under five

A common misconception: "Malaria is an unsolvable fact of the tropics." It was eliminated from the US and Europe, and dozens of countries have been certified malaria-free in our lifetime. It is beatable — it just isn't funded like it.

What the evidence actually points to: Insecticide-treated bed nets — Long-lasting nets that protect sleeping families from mosquito bites. A net costs a few dollars.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/malaria.html
```

### 👶 Child Mortality

```
👶 Child Mortality: Roughly 5 million children under five die each year — most from preventable causes

A common misconception: "Nothing works in these places." Child mortality has fallen faster than almost any other human-welfare metric — because vaccines, oral rehydration and nets demonstrably work.

What the evidence actually points to: Childhood vaccination — Routine immunization against measles, rotavirus, pneumococcus and more. Among the best buys in global health — full immunization costs tens of dollars per child and vaccines are estimated to have saved well over 100 million children since 1974 (WHO).

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/child-mortality.html
```

### 🍚 Hunger & Malnutrition

```
🍚 Hunger & Malnutrition: Roughly 700 million people are undernourished; undernutrition underlies nearly half of child deaths

A common misconception: "There isn't enough food in the world." The world grows more than enough calories for everyone; hunger is about poverty, distribution and conflict — not global scarcity.

What the evidence actually points to: Therapeutic feeding (RUTF) — Ready-to-use peanut-paste foods that let mothers treat severe child malnutrition at home. On the order of $100–200 for a full course of treatment (the peanut paste itself is roughly $50).

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/hunger.html
```

### 💧 Unsafe Water & Sanitation

```
💧 Unsafe Water & Sanitation: Roughly 2 billion people lack safely managed drinking water

A common misconception: "Digging wells solves it." Hardware without maintenance fails within years — the unglamorous work of chlorination, spare parts and fee systems is where impact lives.

What the evidence actually points to: Water chlorination — Dilute chlorine at the point of collection or in supplies — kills the pathogens that kill children. On the order of $1–2 per person per year.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/unsafe-water.html
```

### 📚 Education Gaps

```
📚 Education Gaps: Roughly 250 million children and youth are out of school; most in-school children in poor countries can't read a simple story by age 10

A common misconception: "Building schools is the bottleneck." Buildings and enrollment mostly happened; the hard problem now is what children learn inside them.

What the evidence actually points to: Teaching at the right level — Grouping children by learning level, not age, for part of the day — pioneered in India, replicated widely. A few dollars per child per year in some formats.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/education.html
```

### 💔 Loneliness & Mental Health

```
💔 Loneliness & Mental Health: Roughly 1 in 4 adults worldwide report feeling very or fairly lonely; depression and anxiety are leading causes of disability

A common misconception: "Loneliness is an old-people problem, and mental illness needs psychiatrists to fix." Young people report more loneliness than their grandparents, and trained lay counselors deliver effective therapy at scale.

What the evidence actually points to: Lay-counselor talk therapy — Community members trained to deliver structured therapy (like the "Friendship Bench" model) where psychiatrists are scarce. Tens of dollars per person treated.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/loneliness.html
```

### 🏠 Homelessness

```
🏠 Homelessness: Rough global estimates: ~150 million people homeless; over 1.5 billion in inadequate housing

A common misconception: "People must solve their addiction/illness before they can be housed." Housing First flips this — housing is the stable platform that makes treatment work, and the evidence backs it.

What the evidence actually points to: Housing First — Permanent housing without preconditions, plus voluntary support services. Keeps ~80%+ of chronically homeless people stably housed in trials.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/homelessness.html
```

### 🕊️ Refugees & Displacement

```
🕊️ Refugees & Displacement: Roughly 120 million people are forcibly displaced — the most ever recorded

A common misconception: "Refugees are mostly heading to rich countries and drain their hosts." ~75% are hosted in low- and middle-income countries, and where refugees can legally work, evidence shows they contribute more than they cost over time.

What the evidence actually points to: Cash assistance — Direct cash to displaced families instead of in-kind aid, letting them prioritize their own needs. More efficient than shipping goods in most contexts.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/refugees.html
```

### 🌡️ Climate Change

```
🌡️ Climate Change: The planet is roughly 1.3°C warmer than pre-industrial times; emissions must fall steeply this decade

A common misconception: "It's mainly about personal virtue — recycling, straws, your footprint." Individual footprints matter far less than system change: energy policy, technology cost curves and finance move gigatonnes; personal purity moves kilograms.

What the evidence actually points to: Clean energy deployment & innovation — Solar, wind, storage, grids, electrification — scaling what is now often the cheapest power in history. Cost declines (~90% for solar since 2010) did more for emissions than most policies.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/climate-change.html
```
