## English drafts — Angle A (myth → what works)

### 🌍 Extreme Poverty

```
🌍 Extreme Poverty: Roughly 800 million people live on less than $3.00 a day

A common misconception: "Global poverty keeps getting worse." The opposite: extreme poverty has seen the steepest sustained decline in human history over the last generation. The work is unfinished, not failing.

What the evidence actually points to: Direct cash transfers — Giving money directly to the poorest households, unconditionally. Roughly $1 delivers ~$0.85–0.90 into a family's hands.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/extreme-poverty.html
```

### 🦟 Malaria

```
🦟 Malaria: Roughly 600,000 deaths a year — most of them children under five

A common misconception: "Malaria is an unsolvable fact of the tropics." It was eliminated from the US and Europe, and dozens of countries have been certified malaria-free in our lifetime. It is beatable — it just isn't funded like it.

What the evidence actually points to: Insecticide-treated bed nets — Long-lasting nets that protect sleeping families from mosquito bites. A net costs a few dollars.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/malaria.html
```

### 👶 Child Mortality

```
👶 Child Mortality: Roughly 5 million children under five die each year — most from preventable causes

A common misconception: "Nothing works in these places." Child mortality has fallen faster than almost any other human-welfare metric — because vaccines, oral rehydration and nets demonstrably work.

What the evidence actually points to: Childhood vaccination — Routine immunization against measles, rotavirus, pneumococcus and more. Among the best buys in global health — full immunization costs tens of dollars per child and vaccines are estimated to have saved well over 100 million children since 1974 (WHO).

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/child-mortality.html
```

### 🍚 Hunger & Malnutrition

```
🍚 Hunger & Malnutrition: Roughly 700 million people are undernourished; undernutrition underlies nearly half of child deaths

A common misconception: "There isn't enough food in the world." The world grows more than enough calories for everyone; hunger is about poverty, distribution and conflict — not global scarcity.

What the evidence actually points to: Therapeutic feeding (RUTF) — Ready-to-use peanut-paste foods that let mothers treat severe child malnutrition at home. On the order of $100–200 for a full course of treatment (the peanut paste itself is roughly $50).

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/hunger.html

### 💧 Unsafe Water & Sanitation

```
💧 Unsafe Water & Sanitation: Roughly 2 billion people lack safely managed drinking water

A common misconception: "Digging wells solves it." Hardware without maintenance fails within years — the unglamorous work of chlorination, spare parts and fee systems is where impact lives.

What the evidence actually points to: Water chlorination — Dilute chlorine at the point of collection or in supplies — kills the pathogens that kill children. On the order of $1–2 per person per year.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/unsafe-water.html

### 📚 Education Gaps

```
📚 Education Gaps: Roughly 250 million children and youth are out of school; most in-school children in poor countries can't read a simple story by age 10

A common misconception: "Building schools is the bottleneck." Buildings and enrollment mostly happened; the hard problem now is what children learn inside them.

What the evidence actually points to: Teaching at the right level — Grouping children by learning level, not age, for part of the day — pioneered in India, replicated widely. A few dollars per child per year in some formats.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:

🕊️ Refugees & Displacement: Roughly 120 million people are forcibly displaced — the most ever recorded

A common misconception: "Refugees are mostly heading to rich countries and drain their hosts." ~75% are hosted in low- and middle-income countries, and where refugees can legally work, evidence shows they contribute more than they cost over time.

```
🌡️ Climate Change: The planet is roughly 1.3°C warmer than pre-industrial times; emissions must fall steeply this decade

A common misconception: "It's mainly about personal virtue — recycling, straws, your footprint." Individual footprints matter far less than system change: energy policy, technology cost curves and finance move gigatonnes; personal purity moves kilograms.

What the evidence actually points to: Clean energy deployment & innovation — Solar, wind, storage, grids, electrification — scaling what is now often the cheapest power in history. Cost declines (~90% for solar since 2010) did more for emissions than most policies.

### 💨 Air Pollution

```
💨 Air Pollution: Roughly 7–8 million premature deaths a year — more than malaria, HIV and traffic accidents combined

A common misconception: "Air pollution is an unfortunate nuisance, not a top killer." It kills roughly 10× more people than all wars and murders combined each year, yet receives a sliver of health funding and attention.

What the evidence actually points to: Emission standards & enforcement — Vehicle, power-plant and industrial standards — the policies behind every clean-air success story. The US Clean Air Act's benefits are estimated at ~30× its costs.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/air-pollution.html

### ⚖️ Gender Inequality

```
⚖️ Gender Inequality: Roughly 12 million girls are married as children each year; over 230 million girls and women alive today were cut

A common misconception: "Culture can't change, or changes only over centuries." Child marriage in Bangladesh, cutting in parts of East Africa, and girls' schooling in the Sahel have all shifted measurably within a generation when programs and local movements aligned.

What the evidence actually points to: Keeping girls in school — Scholarships, stipends, uniforms and safe transport that remove the costs pushing girls out. Each extra year of girls' schooling delays marriage, cuts fertility and raises lifetime earnings — among development's best-documented compounding effects.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/gender-inequality.html

### 🐄 Factory Farming

```
🐄 Factory Farming: Roughly 80+ billion land animals are slaughtered yearly — most raised in intensive confinement

A common misconception: "Animal welfare means being vegan or nothing." Corporate welfare campaigns — cage bans, slower-growing breeds — have improved hundreds of millions of lives per year, funded by donors who mostly still eat meat.

What the evidence actually points to: Corporate welfare campaigns — Pressuring food companies to commit to cage-free eggs and better broiler standards, then holding them to it. Analyses estimate a dollar can improve multiple animal-years of life — plausibly the most suffering reduced per dollar anywhere in philanthropy.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/factory-farming.html

### 👁️ Preventable Blindness

👁️ Preventable Blindness: Roughly 1 billion people have vision impairment that could be prevented or corrected

A common misconception: "Blindness is mostly incurable fate." More than half of it is solved by two of medicine's most routine fixes: eyeglasses and cataract surgery.

What the evidence actually points to: Cataract surgery programs — High-volume, low-cost surgery camps and eye hospitals restoring sight in minutes. Roughly $25–100 per surgery in efficient programs — sight restored for the price of a dinner out.

🦠 Pandemic Preparedness: COVID-19 caused roughly 20–28 million excess deaths; the next pandemic is a matter of when

### 🫁 Tuberculosis

```
🫁 Tuberculosis: Roughly 1.25 million deaths a year — the world's deadliest infectious disease

A common misconception: "TB is a disease of the past." It kills over a million people every year, quietly, in poor places — it is curable with roughly six months of antibiotics costing tens of dollars.

What the evidence actually points to: Active case finding + treatment support — Going out to find undiagnosed cases (X-ray vans, contact tracing) and supporting patients through the full drug course. Treatment cures ~85%+ of drug-sensitive cases.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/tuberculosis.html

### 🧪 Lead Poisoning

```
🧪 Lead Poisoning: Roughly 1 in 3 children worldwide has blood lead levels high enough to harm their development

A common misconception: "Lead was solved when leaded gasoline was banned." Petrol was only one source; paint, batteries and contaminated food still poison hundreds of millions of children today.

What the evidence actually points to: Lead paint regulation — Passing and enforcing bans on lead in paint — many countries still allow it. Advocacy for paint laws is astonishingly cheap per child protected.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/lead-poisoning.html

### 🤰 Maternal Mortality

```
🤰 Maternal Mortality: Roughly 260,000–290,000 women die each year from pregnancy and childbirth — almost all preventably

A common misconception: "Childbirth is just inherently dangerous." Rich countries cut maternal deaths by ~99% over a century. Every solution is known; what's missing is access.

What the evidence actually points to: Skilled birth attendance + emergency obstetric care — Trained midwives at every birth, with transport and referral to facilities that can do transfusions and C-sections. The backbone of every country that has driven deaths down.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/maternal-mortality.html

### 🚗 Road Traffic Deaths

```
🚗 Road Traffic Deaths: Roughly 1.2 million deaths a year — the leading killer of people aged 5 to 29

A common misconception: "Crashes are accidents — careless individuals." System design predicts deaths far better than driver virtue: countries that engineered safety (speeds, road design, vehicle standards) cut deaths by 50–80% while driving more than ever.

What the evidence actually points to: Speed management + safe road design — Lower urban limits, speed bumps, roundabouts, pedestrian crossings and separated lanes. A 5% cut in average speed reduces deaths ~30%.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/road-deaths.html

### 🚬 Tobacco

```
🚬 Tobacco: Roughly 8 million deaths a year — the world's leading preventable cause of death

A common misconception: "Smoking is yesterday's problem, basically solved." It still kills more people than any infectious disease, and this century's projected toll is measured in hundreds of millions of lives if trends hold.

What the evidence actually points to: Tobacco taxation — Raising cigarette prices through excise tax — the WHO's single most effective "best buy". A 10% price rise cuts consumption ~4–8%, most among the young and poor.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/tobacco.html

### 🎗️ HIV / AIDS

```
🎗️ HIV / AIDS: Roughly 630,000 deaths a year, and about 40 million people living with HIV

A common misconception: "HIV is either a death sentence or a solved problem." Neither: a person on treatment has near-normal life expectancy and cannot transmit the virus (undetectable = untransmittable) — yet hundreds of thousands still die yearly purely for lack of access.

What the evidence actually points to: Antiretroviral therapy at scale — Daily pills that suppress the virus to undetectable — the person stays healthy and cannot pass it on. Generic first-line treatment costs well under $100 per person-year.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/hiv-aids.html
```

### 🪱 Neglected Tropical Diseases

```
🪱 Neglected Tropical Diseases: Over 1 billion people are affected by diseases most people have never heard of

A common misconception: "Rare, exotic diseases." They afflict over a billion people — more than almost any famous disease — they're just invisible from rich countries because they rarely kill quickly; they disable instead.

What the evidence actually points to: Mass drug administration — Community-wide annual doses of safe, donated drugs that treat and prevent several NTDs at once. Often well under $1 per person per year (drugs are donated by manufacturers.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/neglected-tropical-diseases.html
```

### 📶 Digital Exclusion

```
📶 Digital Exclusion: Roughly 2.6 billion people — a third of humanity — remain offline

A common misconception: "The internet is basically everywhere now." A third of humanity has never been online; assuming universal connectivity quietly designs them out of modern life.

What the evidence actually points to: Affordability policy & competition — Spectrum policy, competition and targeted subsidies that push data and device prices down. Where prices fell below ~2% of monthly income, adoption followed.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/digital-exclusion.html
```

### 🏛️ Corruption

```
🏛️ Corruption: Bribery alone is estimated at roughly $1 trillion a year; corruption drains several percent of global GDP

A common misconception: "Corruption is culture — some places are just like that." Georgia, Estonia and others cut corruption drastically in years, not generations, by redesigning systems (digitize services, remove discretion, publish everything) rather than preaching virtue.

What the evidence actually points to: Digitizing government services — Moving permits, payments and records online, removing the human gatekeeper a bribe would go to. E-government reforms repeatedly show large drops in petty bribery — the design removes the opportunity rather than testing the morals.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/corruption.html
```

### 🌊 Ocean Health

```
🌊 Ocean Health: Roughly 8–11 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean each year; over a third of fish stocks are overfished

A common misconception: "The fix is cleaning up the garbage patch." Cleanup is the least effective end of the pipe: stopping flows — river interception, waste systems, fishing-gear rules — prevents far more per dollar, and roughly half the Great Pacific patch by mass is fishing gear, not straws.

What the evidence actually points to: Marine protected areas with real enforcement — No-take zones where ecosystems rebuild. Fish biomass typically rebounds several-fold inside enforced reserves and spills over to surrounding fisheries — protection pays fishers back.

Understand it in three minutes, and see concrete ways to help:
https://tools.panoskokmotos.com/compass/p/ocean-health.html
```
